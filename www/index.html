About Project BOOST

Project BOOST, funded by the State Farm Youth Advisory Board, is a multi-disciplinary research, education and outreach program that provides service-learning opportunities for students. The grant supports the training of students and teachers and the purchase of equipment and other supplies needed for the research initiative.

Hood College faculty, staff and students in the environmental and computer science programs, in partnership with Frederick High School and Frederick County Public School's Earth and Space Science Lab, are monitoring the temperature of streams in the Tuscarora watershed, and identifying those that maintain the optimal temperature and are the best potential habitats for Eastern Brook Trout restoration efforts. Frederick High School students enrolled in environmental science classes are raising trout in their classrooms and releasing them into a suitable stream.

Due to rapid urbanization, water temperatures in many tributaries within the Tuscarora watershed have increased, which has led to a decline in Eastern Brook Trout populations there. Future plans include stream restoration based on the results of these studies.
